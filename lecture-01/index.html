Syntax

The set of rules, principles, and processes that govern the structure of sentences.

Part-of-Speech Tagging

DT  NN   VBD   IN  DT  NN
the girl walked to  the park

Key:
DT = determiner, NN = noun, VBD = verb (past tense), IN = preposition

Constituency Parsing

Tree structure showing sentence composition (S, NP, VP, PP)

Dependency Parsing

Key:
DET = determiner, NSUBJ = nominal subject, ROOT = root of the sentence,
PREP = prepositional modifier, POBJ = object of a preposition

Semantics

Meaning at the levels of words, phrases, sentences, or larger units of discourse.

Semantic Role Labeling

yesterday at the park the man gave crumbs to the birds

ARG0 = usually agent
ARG1 = typically patient or theme
ARG2 = often beneficiary

Coreference Resolution

The boy threw some bread to a group of birds. They fought over it as he watched.

  • Cluster 0: a group of birds, They
  • Cluster 1: some bread, it
  • Cluster 2: The boy, he

Winograd Schema Coreference Resolution

The man couldn't lift his son because he was so weak.

→ "he" refers to man


The man couldn't lift his son because he was so heavy.

→ "he" refers to son

Natural Language Inference

Sentence A: Yesterday's game was canceled because of the heavy rains.

Sentence B Relationship
It rained yesterday. A entails B
It didn't rain yesterday. B contradicts A
I cleaned my basement. A and B are neutral

Reading Comprehension Question Answering

Once there was a boy named Fritz who loved to draw. He drew everything. In the morning, he drew a picture of his cereal with milk. His papa said, "Don't draw your cereal. Eat it!" After school, Fritz drew a picture of his bicycle. His uncle said, "Don't draw your bicycle. Ride it!" ...

What did Fritz draw first?

  • A) the toothpaste
  • B) his mama
  • C) cereal and milk ✓
  • D) his bicycle

Example from MCTest (Richardson et al., 2013)

Why is NLP hard?

Ambiguity

one form can have multiple meanings

Variability

multiple forms can have the same meaning

Example: Hyperlinks in Wikipedia

Ambiguity: "bar" can link to:

  • bar (law)
  • bar (establishment)
  • bar association
  • bar (unit)
  • medal bar
  • bar (music)

Variability: "bar (establishment)" can be referenced as:

  • bar, bars, saloon, saloons, lounge, pub, sports bar...

Why is NLP hard?

  • Analysis: NL → ℛ
  • Generation: ℛ → NL
  • ℛ can be a theorized construct, not directly observable.
